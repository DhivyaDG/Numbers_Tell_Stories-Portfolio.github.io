Life Expectancy and Income: A Clear Link to Regional Prosperity

This project examines regional trends in population, life expectancy and income levels from 1800 to 2010, emphasizing the differences in income brackets and their effects on regional demographics. It highlights the correlation between higher income groups and longer life expectancy and also explores the economic and demographic evolution of these regions, with particular attention to the relationship between GDP growth and health outcomes.
